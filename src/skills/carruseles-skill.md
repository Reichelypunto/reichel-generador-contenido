# Carruseles — Viral Instagram Copy Generator v2.0

## Purpose

Generate Instagram carousels that are "difficiles de ignorar" (impossible to ignore) by integrating psychological profiling, proven viral hook formulas, strategic narrative patterns, 11-element validation, and flexible export options. This SKILL transforms any content (transcriptions, articles, emails, summaries) into high-converting carousel copy that drives engagement, saves, and shares.

Unlike generic carousel builders, this system:
- Identifies psychological triggers specific to your audience
- Applies 10 proven viral hook formulas (not generic "curiosity gaps")
- Uses 3 execution styles matched to user profile (Coach → Negativo, Vendedor → Info Secreta, Creador → Controversial)
- Validates all 11 critical elements before delivery
- Generates batches (5-10 carousels) with intelligent style distribution
- Exports to DOCX (shareable with design teams), Notion (calendar automation), or both

---

## Input Requirements

**Base Content** (required)
- Transcription of video, article, email, text summary, or any structured information
- Topic/theme clarity (what problem does this solve? What transformation does it promise?)

**User Profile** (recommended)
- Coach, Vendedor (seller), Educador (educator), Creador (creator), or Experto (expert)
- Will default to Controversial if not specified

**Optional Flags:**
- `--style=Negativo|Info-Secreta|Controversial` → Force specific execution style
- `--batch=5-10` → Generate 5 or 10 different carousels with style distribution
- `--export=docx|notion|both` → Output format (default: markdown table)
- `--include-visuals` → Add detailed visual direction for each slide
- `--multiple-angles` → Generate 3-5 different narrative approaches
- `--slides=7-12` → Override default 10-slide structure (rarely needed)

## STEP 1: INSIGHT EXTRACTION

Extract the core emotional and strategic insights. Don't list visibly—use to build better carousel storytelling.

### Key Questions to Answer:

1. **What problem does this content touch?**
   - Surface problem (e.g., "I'm not selling enough")
   - Real problem beneath (e.g., "My message doesn't resonate because I'm not specific enough")

2. **What tension exists?**
   - Gap between what they believe and reality?
   - Pain they're tolerating without realizing?
   - Contradiction in their approach?

3. **What phrase can open strong?**
   - One-liner that makes them stop scrolling
   - Should attack the problem or create curiosity gap
   - Examples: "Tu problema no es vender. Es que nadie entiende por qué escucharte." / "Estás educando gratis"

4. **What transformation is promised?**
   - Not vague ("be better") but specific (e.g., "Move from chasing clients to having them chase you")

5. **What idea will make them say "mierda, esto me pasa"?**
   - The self-recognition moment
   - The confession they've been avoiding
   - The pattern they finally see

6. **What final question can provoke comments?**
   - Uncomfortable, easy to answer, opens conversation
   - Examples: "¿Qué excusa vas a dejar hoy?" / "¿Dónde estás perdiendo clientes sin darte cuenta?"

## STEP 3: SCRIPT EXECUTION STYLES

Carousels are structured according to your profile. Each style has a unique progression and emotional rhythm.

### NEGATIVO (20-35 slides for carousel context; typically 8-10 slides)

**When to use:** Coach, transformation content, attacking mindset/habits

**Structure:**
1. **Error/Problem** → State the mistake or mindset that's costing them
2. **Consequence** → What happens when they keep doing this
3. **Why They Do It** → The excuse or belief they're hiding behind
4. **The Correction** → What to do instead
5. **Proof/Example** → How it works in reality
6. **Transformation** → The result when they switch

**Tone:** Direct, uncomfortable, specific. Not harsh—sharp. Cuts through the noise.

**Example progression:**
- Slide 1: "Tu problema no es vender. Es que nadie entiende por qué escucharte."
- Slide 2: "Cuando no comunicas diferente, compites por precio"
- Slide 3: "Y compitiendo por precio, pierdes dinero y energía"
- Slides 4-8: The shift (desde... hacia...)
- Slide 10: "¿Qué parte de tu comunicación vas a cambiar esta semana?"

---

### INFO SECRETA (30-50 slides in long-form; typically 10 slides for carousel)

**When to use:** Vendedor, Educador, reveal hidden knowledge

**Structure:**
1. **Setup** → Context or common belief
2. **Surprise** → The hidden info / what everyone misses
3. **Explanation** → Why this is true / how it works
4. **Application** → How to use this insight (step-by-step if needed)
5. **Result** → What changes when you know this

**Tone:** Confidential, revealing, authority-based. Like you're telling them something their competitors don't want known.

**Example progression:**
- Slide 1: "Tu cliente no compra por el producto. Compra por la conversación que tiene en su cabeza."
- Slide 2: "Y esa conversación la controlas TÚ desde el primer mensaje"
- Slides 3-8: The mechanism (cómo tu mensaje shapes their internal dialogue)
- Slide 10: "¿Qué conversación está teniendo tu cliente ahora mismo?"

---

### CONTROVERSIAL (25-45 slides in depth; typically 10 slides for carousel)

**When to use:** Creador, Experto, challenge beliefs, stand out

**Structure:**
1. **Belief** → State the widely-held belief (the one everyone trusts)
2. **Challenge** → Question or attack it directly
3. **Evidence** → Proof the belief is wrong/limited
4. **Alternative** → Your contrarian position
5. **Invitation** → Call them to think differently

**Tone:** Provocative, intellectual, bold. Not controversial for shock—for clarity.

**Example progression:**
- Slide 1: "El engagement no vende. Ni seguidores. Ni algoritmo."
- Slide 2: "Entonces, ¿por qué estás obsesionado con eso?"
- Slides 3-8: What actually moves the needle
- Slide 10: "¿Estás jugando el juego equivocado?"

## STEP 4: CARRUSEL GENERATION

**Default Structure: 10 Slides**
(Override with `--slides=7-12` only if necessary)

### Slide 1: THE HOOK
- **Max 5 words** (absolute ceiling—aim for 3-4)
- Function: Stop scroll, create tension or curiosity
- Must be provocative, specific, and emotionally loaded
- Not explanatory—it's bait

**Bad:** "Cómo mejorar tus ventas"  
**Good:** "Tu problema no es vender"

### Slides 2-9: DEVELOPMENT (8 slides)
- **20-45 words per slide** (varies by content, rhythm matters)
- One idea per slide
- Each slide must push to the next
- No filler, no corporate speak, no generic motivation
- Storytelling, not summarizing

**Rhythm:**
- Slide 2: Open/context/expansion of Slide 1
- Slides 3-6: Core message (step-by-step, narrative, evidence, examples)
- Slides 7-8: Shift/transformation/proof
- Slide 9: Bridge to final question

### Slide 10: THE QUESTION
- Function: Generate comments, close conversation loop, not end it
- Uncomfortable, easy to answer, personal
- Not "Follow for more" or generic CTA (that goes in caption)
- Forces them to reflect or commit

**Good questions:**
- "¿Qué excusa vas a dejar hoy?"
- "¿Dónde estás perdiendo clientes sin darte cuenta?"
- "¿Sigues esperando permiso?"
- "¿Qué te duele más: vender poco o no intentar?"

| **Caption Estratégico** | ✓ ✗ | Caption refuerza gancho, añade contexto, y tiene CTA claro (guardar, comentar, DM, etc.) |

## STEP 7: BATCH GENERATION & EXPORT OPTIONS

### BATCH MODE
Generate 5-10 complete carousels on the same topic with different approaches/styles.

**`--batch=5`**
- 5 carousels, distributed intelligently:
  - 1 × Info Secreta (education/revelation angle)
  - 1 ×  Negativo (error/correction angle)
  - 1 × Controversial (challenge belief angle)
  - 2 × variations of the strongest approach

**`--batch=10`**
- 10 carousels, distributed 40/35/25:
  - 4 × Info Secreta (40%—highest engagement/saves)
  - 3.5 → 4 × Negativo (35%—strong transformation appeal)
  - 2.5 → 2 × Controversial (25%—differentiation)

Each carousel has a distinct:
- Opening hook (different formula from the 10)
- Narrative pattern (varied to prevent repetition)
- Emotional emphasis (some pain-heavy, some desire-heavy)
- Caption & CTA (tailored to the angle)

---

### EXPORT OPTIONS

**Default: `--export=markdown`**
```
| Slide | Copy |
|-------|------|
| 1 | Hook |
| ... | ... |
```

**`--export=docx`**
- Individual .docx files per carousel
- Naming: `[Topic]_Carousel-[Number]_[Style].docx`
- Each file includes:
  - Carousel table (Slide | Copy)
  - Caption (if generated)
  - 11-element validation checklist
  - Notes for designer (visual direction if requested)
  - Performance tracking fields (hook, style, narrative, motor viral)

**`--export=notion`**
- Creates Notion database: `"[Topic] — Carousel Calendar"`
- One page per carousel
- Properties: Slide, Copy, Style, Hook Formula, Narrative Pattern, Motor Viral, Caption, CTA, Validation Status
- Ready to assign dates and schedule posting

**`--export=both`**
- DOCX files (for design teams)
- Notion database (for scheduling + performance tracking)
- CSV export (for Google Sheets or other workflows)

**`--include-visuals`**
- Adds visual direction column to each slide
- Format: context, emotion/expression, framing, background, lighting, symbolic element, feeling

Example:
```
Slide 1 Visual Direction:
"Person sitting at laptop looking frustrated, phone with notifications ignored, harsh desk lighting, close-up on face showing internal conflict. Feeling: stalled."
```

## Final Notes

1. **Content quality in = quality carousel out.** If your input is vague, the carousel will be generic. Be specific about the problem, transformation, or revelation.

2. **The hook is everything.** If Slide 1 doesn't stop someone, nothing else matters. Spend time getting this right.

3. **Batch generation is your weapon.** One carousel might flop. Five carousels with different hooks/angles increase odds exponentially. Use `--batch=10` for serious campaigns.

4. **Validation matters.** A carousel that passes all 11 elements converts better, generates more engagement, and builds authority. Don't ship without it.

5. **Performance data is feedback.** Track which formulas, styles, and patterns work. Use that to inform your next batches.

6. **Carousel ≠ Summary.** You're not summarizing content. You're creating a standalone experience that makes people *think, feel, and act*. If it sounds like a cliff notes version of the original, you've missed the point.
